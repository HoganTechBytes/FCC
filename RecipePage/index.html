Viking Flatbread

This Viking flatbread is so flavorful. Using flours the Vikings might have used—spelt flour and rye flour—and moistening the dough with buttermilk rather than water, the flatbreads have a bit of a sourdough note.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/3 cup spelt flour
  • 1/4 cup rye flour
  • 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  • 3/4 cup buttermilk

Instructions

  1. Step one: Combine flour, spelt flour, rye flour, and salt in a bowl and stir with a wooden spoon to combine.
  2. Step two: Pour in buttermilk, and stir until a shaggy dough forms. Clean off the spoon and work the mixture with your hands until it comes together into a ball of dough, and the sides of the bowl are clean.
  3. Step three: Transfer onto a work surface, and using only enough flour to prevent sticking, knead until a smooth, elastic, and slightly sticky dough forms, about 2 minutes. Cover dough and let it rest for 15 minutes at room temperature.
  4. Step four: Divide into 6 equal portions and roll each one into a ball. Using just enough flour to prevent sticking, roll each portion out into a circle about 1/8-inch thick.
  5. Step five: Heat a dry cast iron pan over medium-high heat. Place in flatbread and cook until browned and blistered, about 1 to 1 1/2 minutes per side.
  6. Step six: Pile up cooked flatbreads on a plate and cover with a dry kitchen towel as they are being made. Let rest, covered, for about 10 minutes before serving.
